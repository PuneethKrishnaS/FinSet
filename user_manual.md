# Personal Expense Tracker: User Manual

Welcome to your new Personal Expense Tracker! This application is designed to be your all-in-one financial command center. Whether you're trying to save up for a big purchase, figure out where your paycheck goes every month, or just keep track of money you lent to a friend, this platform makes it incredibly visual and easy to manage.

Here is a complete guide to everything you can do in the application:

---

## 1. The Dashboard (Your Financial Command Center)
Whenever you log in, you are greeted by the **Dashboard**. This is designed to give you an instant snapshot of your financial health without requiring you to click around.

**What you can do here:**
* **Check Your Bottom Line:** At the very top, you'll see four critical numbers: your Total Balance, how much you've made this month (Income), how much you've spent (Expenses), and your Net Savings.
* **Analyze Cash Flow:** Look at the large graph in the center. The green line represents your income over the month, and the red line represents your spending. If the red line spikes above the green, you know you're spending too fast!
* **View the Transaction Calendar:** Click the **"View Calendar"** button in the top right. A huge, Google-style calendar will pop up. You can scroll through individual days to see exactly what you bought on a specific Tuesday, complete with daily total summaries.
* **Spot Spending Trends:** At the bottom left, a beautiful pie chart breaks down your spending into categories (like "Food" vs "Transport"), complete with percentages.
* **Monitor Budgets:** At the bottom right, you'll see quick progress bars for your active budgets, warning you if you are close to hitting a limit.

---

## 2. Transactions (Logging Money In & Out)
The **Transactions** tab is where the magic happens. This is where you tell the app about your daily financial activities.

**What you can do here:**
* **Log an Expense:** Bought a coffee? Paid rent? Type in the amount, add a quick description (like "Starbucks"), select the exact date, and click the relevant category icon (e.g., the little coffee cup). Hit save, and your dashboard updates instantly.
* **Log an Income:** Got your paycheck? Sold something online? Switch to the "Income" tab, enter the amount, and categorize it as "Salary" or "Investment." 
* **See Recent Activity:** As soon as you log a transaction, it pops up immediately on the right side of the screen in your "Recent Activity" feed so you can confirm it went through.

---

## 3. History (Searching & Exporting)
The **History** tab acts as your personal financial filing cabinet. It contains a beautiful, scrollable list of every single transaction you have ever made.

**What you can do here:**
* **Search for Anything:** Can't remember how much you paid for that Netflix subscription three months ago? Just type "Netflix" into the search bar, and the list will filter instantly.
* **Dynamic Filters:** Click the "Income" or "Expenses" pill buttons to filter the list. If you click Expenses, a new row of icons appears allowing you to filter even further by specific categories (like seeing *only* your "Transport" costs).
* **Live Summaries:** As you search or filter, the three summary cards at the top of the page ("Filtered Income", "Filtered Expenses", "Net Flow") update in real-time to show you the exact math of what's currently on your screen!
* **Export Your Data:** Need to send your history to an accountant or view it in Excel? Click the **"Export CSV"** button to instantly download your entire history as a spreadsheet.

---

## 4. Budgets (Setting Goals & Limits)
The **Budgets** tab is designed to stop you from overspending before it happens. 

**What you can do here:**
* **Create Spending Limits:** Decide how much you want to spend on a specific category for the month (for example, a $300 limit for "Food").
* **Visual Tracking:** Once a budget is set, it creates a beautiful circular progress ring. As you log transactions throughout the month, the ring fills up. 
* **Warning System:** If you accidentally spend $350 on Food, your $300 progress ring will immediately turn red, instantly alerting you that you've blown past your goal.
* **Month Overview:** A sidebar tracks exactly how many days are left in the current month, calculating your "Total Budgeted" money against your "Total Spent" so you know how much breathing room you have left.

---

## 5. Debts (Tracking IOU's)
Do you often spot your friends for dinner, or borrow money to cover a bill? The **Debts** tab ensures you never lose track of an IOU again.

**What you can do here:**
* **Track Money Lent:** Log when you loan money to someone. The app will subtract this from your total "Available Balance" on the dashboard because that money has left your pocket.
* **Track Money Borrowed:** Log when you owe someone money.
* **Settle Up:** When a friend finally pays you back, simply click the **"Mark Settled"** button next to their name. The app crosses out the debt and automatically recalculates your dashboard balances to reflect that the money has returned to your wallet!

---

**In Summary:**
This platform isn't just a digital ledger; it's an interactive assistant. Every time you log a transaction, set a budget, or lend money, the app's charts, calendars, and progress rings update dynamically to give you a perfect, real-time picture of your financial life.
